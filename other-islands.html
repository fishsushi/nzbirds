Other Islands

Some species, like the Takahe, are found mainly on predator-free island sanctuaries. These sanctuaries are closely monitored for predators to ensure that protected animals aren't getting attacked. it is important for protected species to have sanctuaries free from predators so that they do not go extinct. One of the most well-known protected birds, the Takahe, was thought to be extinct, but was rediscovered and has been protected ever since.

Offshore Islands

Here are some NZ islands where endangered birds inhabit:

  • Tiritiri Matangi Scientific Reserve
  • Stewart Island
  • Chatham Islands

What birds can be found on NZ islands?

There are many birds who inhabit offshore islands near the country, some are:

  • Blue Penguin
  • Stitchbird
  • Tui
  • Wood Pigeon
  • NZ Plover
  • Morepork
  • Bellbird

How are these birds protected?

The people who take care of these islands and sanctuaries use different measures to keep these animals safe. The main pests who threaten these species are kept off the island by making sure that they havent sneaked onto boats or inside luggage. They also perform regular checks on the birds' health and have ink pads to monitor what animals are roaming around. Predators like rats and possums are not on the island, making it a safe environment for birds to breed and lay eggs.
