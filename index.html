New Zealand is home to various species of birds. From the ancient moa to more recent species, NZ has dense bush and unique birds. With so many birds who are endemic to the country, it is important that extra care is taken so they do not become extinct.

What does endemic mean?

Endemic species are species that can only be found in a specific place or country. For example, if an endemic animal goes extinct in the one country that it's in, that means that it is gone from the entire world. Many endemic birds in NZ are endangered, meaning they are at risk of extinction.

Which birds are at most risk?

Here are some, but not all, endangered birds:

  • Kakapo
  • Black Stilt
  • Little Spotted Kiwi
  • Black Robin
  • Brown Teal
  • Blue Duck
  • Chatham Parakeet

What threats do they face?

Did you know that around 1.12 million native birds are killed every year by house cats?

Native birds are threatened by:

  • House cat attacks
  • Littering
  • Hunting
  • Climate change
  • Pests
  • Invasive species, etc.

Many New Zealand birds have gone extinct in the past, like the Moa and Laughing owl. In order to make sure other birds don't go extinct, there's lots you can do to help.
