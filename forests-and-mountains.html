Forests and Mountains

In the forests of New Zealand, you can find birds pecking at roots and chattering up in the canopy of trees. Every morning a dawn chorus can be heard coming from a variety of different birds. Birds are an important part of the forest ecosystem and help maintain a healthy food web.


Emerging Threats

As safe as this habitat once was, invasive species have found their way in and are ruining the balance.

Common Invasive Species

Most species that currently pose a threat to native species have come from overseas. An example of this is the possum. They were brought from other countries using boats and have been a threat ever since. Many pests kill both eggs and adult birds. Here are some common invasive species to the forests and mountains of NZ:

  • Brushtail Possum
  • Common Goat
  • Highland Hare
  • Fallow Deer
  • Weasels and stoats
  • Wild Pigs


Birds at Risk

Here are some of the endangered species that inhabit the alpine mountains and bushland of New Zealand:

  • Rowi
  • New Zealand Robin
  • Great Spotted Kiwi
  • Rifleman
  • Bush Falcon
  • Kea
  • Kakapo
